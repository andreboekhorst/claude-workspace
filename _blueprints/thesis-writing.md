# Thesis Writing

**Follow-up:** What is your thesis topic?

## Skills

### Research
Literature review, source gathering, and gap analysis.
1. Define search scope and key terms
2. Summarize relevant papers and findings
3. Map the landscape — agreements, debates, gaps
4. Identify where your work fits

In: research question, PDFs of papers, reading notes | Out: literature review → `files/sources/`

### Plan
Structure the thesis — chapters, arguments, timeline to defense.
1. Define the central argument and sub-claims
2. Map chapters with key points per chapter
3. Set writing milestones and deadlines
4. Flag dependencies — data, advisor feedback, revisions

In: research question, literature review, defense date | Out: thesis plan → `files/notes/`

### Draft
Write chapters from outlines with citations.
1. Expand outline into full prose
2. Integrate evidence and citations
3. Maintain academic voice and logical flow
4. Mark sections needing data or revision

In: chapter outline, sources, images of diagrams | Out: chapter draft → `files/chapters/`

### Edit
Revise for academic rigor, clarity, and argument coherence.
1. Check argument structure — does each section advance the thesis?
2. Tighten prose — eliminate jargon bloat and hedging
3. Verify citations and evidence support claims
4. Flag logical gaps or unsupported assertions

In: chapter draft | Out: revised chapter → `files/chapters/`

### Synthesize
Weave findings across chapters into a cohesive argument.
1. Extract key findings from each chapter
2. Identify cross-chapter patterns and tensions
3. Draft synthesis — how parts build the whole
4. Write conclusion and implications

In: chapter drafts, research question | Out: synthesis draft → `files/chapters/`

## Tools
- **WebSearch** — find papers, check citations, explore related work

## Folders
- `files/chapters/` — chapter drafts and revisions
- `files/sources/` — literature reviews, paper summaries, PDFs
- `files/notes/` — outlines, plans, advisor feedback
