# Fiction Writing

**Follow-up:** What are you working on — novel, short story, script?

## Skills

### Brainstorm
Generate story concepts, premises, and what-if scenarios.
1. Clarify genre, tone, and length
2. Generate a batch of premises or hooks
3. Explore the strongest ideas with character and conflict sketches
4. Narrow to one concept with a clear through-line

In: genre, themes, inspiration | Out: story concepts → `files/notes/`

### Draft
Write scenes and chapters from outlines or prompts.
1. Review outline, prior scenes, and voice notes
2. Establish scene goal, POV, and tension
3. Draft the scene or chapter
4. Flag continuity questions for later

In: outline, character notes, prior chapters | Out: draft scenes → `files/drafts/`

### Edit
Revise for voice, pacing, and consistency.
1. Read the draft end-to-end, noting friction points
2. Check voice consistency and character behavior
3. Tighten pacing — cut filler, sharpen transitions
4. Output a clean revision with a change summary

In: draft text | Out: revised draft → `files/drafts/`

### Research
World-building research and setting details.
1. Identify what needs grounding — setting, tech, culture, history
2. Gather relevant details and sensory specifics
3. Organize into a reference sheet
4. Flag anything that needs the author's creative decision

In: story concept, setting questions | Out: reference notes → `files/world-building/`

### Critique
Self-critique structure, character arcs, and plot holes.
1. Summarize the current story state
2. Test each character arc for motivation and payoff
3. Check plot logic — cause/effect, foreshadowing, gaps
4. Deliver honest notes with specific fix suggestions

In: draft or outline | Out: critique notes → `files/notes/`

## Tools
None required.

## Folders
- `files/drafts/` — scenes, chapters, manuscripts
- `files/notes/` — brainstorms, critiques, loose ideas
- `files/world-building/` — setting details, character sheets, timelines
