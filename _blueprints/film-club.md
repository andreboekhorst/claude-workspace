# Film Club

**Follow-up:** What draws you to film, and is this solo or a group club?

## Skills

### Research
Explore director filmographies, film context, and critical reception.
1. Define the film or filmmaker to research
2. Gather background — production history, influences, reception
3. Summarize key themes and critical perspectives
4. Note connections to other works

In: film title, director name | Out: research notes → `files/discussions/`

### Draft
Write film reviews or discussion guides.
1. Clarify format — review, essay, discussion questions
2. Structure around a central argument or theme
3. Draft with specific references to scenes and techniques
4. Edit for insight and readability

In: film notes, screenshots of scenes | Out: review or guide → `files/reviews/`

### Compare
Evaluate films across themes, techniques, or directors.
1. Define the comparison axis
2. Analyze each film against shared criteria
3. Highlight similarities, contrasts, and evolutions
4. Draw conclusions about patterns or preferences

In: film notes, watchlist | Out: comparative analysis → `files/discussions/`

### Synthesize
Find patterns across a series of films watched.
1. Review notes from multiple viewings
2. Identify recurring themes, techniques, or preoccupations
3. Map connections and evolution across films
4. Draft a synthesis essay or reflection

In: reviews, discussion notes | Out: synthesis essay → `files/discussions/`

### Brainstorm
Curate themed watchlists.
1. Define the theme, genre, or question to explore
2. Generate candidates — deep cuts and essentials
3. Sequence for variety and thematic build
4. Add brief notes on why each film fits

In: theme, past viewings, preferences | Out: watchlist → `files/watchlists/`

## Tools
- **WebSearch** — look up filmographies, reviews, streaming availability

## Folders
- `files/reviews/` — film reviews, essays, personal ratings
- `files/watchlists/` — themed lists, queues, recommendations
- `files/discussions/` — discussion guides, research notes, syntheses
