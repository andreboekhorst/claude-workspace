# Book Writing

**Follow-up:** What's your book about, and who's the audience?

## Skills

### Plan
Book structure — thesis, chapters, and arc.
1. Clarify the book's core argument and audience
2. Draft a chapter outline with key points per chapter
3. Map the narrative arc — setup, build, payoff
4. Identify where evidence and stories are needed

In: book concept, audience notes | Out: book outline → `files/outlines/`

### Research
Gather sources, evidence, and case studies.
1. Identify what each chapter needs to prove or illustrate
2. Gather relevant sources, data, and examples
3. Summarize each source with key quotes and page refs
4. Organize by chapter for easy retrieval

In: chapter outline, source PDFs, articles, links | Out: research notes → `files/research/`

### Draft
Write chapters from outlines.
1. Review chapter outline and supporting research
2. Write a first draft with clear argument flow
3. Weave in evidence, stories, and examples
4. Flag gaps where more research or a better example is needed

In: chapter outline, research notes | Out: chapter draft → `files/manuscript/`

### Edit
Revise for clarity, flow, and argument strength.
1. Read the chapter end-to-end for coherence
2. Tighten prose — cut redundancy, sharpen transitions
3. Verify claims are supported and examples land
4. Output a clean revision with a change summary

In: chapter draft | Out: revised chapter → `files/manuscript/`

### Synthesize
Weave multiple chapters into a coherent narrative.
1. Read across chapters for consistency and flow
2. Check that the through-line builds without repetition
3. Smooth transitions between chapters
4. Flag structural issues — reordering, merging, splitting

In: multiple chapter drafts | Out: synthesis notes → `files/outlines/`

## Tools
- **WebSearch** — find sources, verify facts, discover case studies

## Folders
- `files/manuscript/` — chapter drafts and revisions
- `files/research/` — source summaries, PDFs, reference notes
- `files/outlines/` — book structure, chapter outlines, synthesis notes
