# Plan Content

Build a content calendar that maps topics, formats, and publication dates to strategic goals.

## Typical flow

1. **Define** goals — clarify what the content plan should achieve (awareness, engagement, sales, education).
2. **Brainstorm** topics — generate topic ideas aligned with goals, audience interests, and available expertise.
3. **Sequence** and schedule — arrange topics into a logical order and assign target dates.
4. **Assign** formats — choose the right format for each piece (blog, video, newsletter, social post, etc.).
5. **Review** the plan — check for balance, pacing, and coverage gaps before finalizing.
6. **Deliver** the content calendar as a structured, scannable document.

## Inputs

- Goals for the content (what success looks like).
- Target audience and channels.
- Time frame (e.g., "next 4 weeks", "Q2 2026").
- Existing content or themes to build on (optional).
- Constraints — publishing frequency, team capacity, budget.

## Outputs

- A content calendar listing each piece with: topic, format, target date, and brief description.
- Optional: theme groupings or content pillars that tie the plan together.

## Quality signals

- Every piece ties back to a stated goal — no orphan topics.
- The schedule is realistic given the stated constraints.
- There is variety in format and topic to maintain audience interest.
- The plan has a logical arc — it builds momentum rather than feeling random.
- Dates account for lead time, review cycles, and dependencies.

## Common variations

- **Single-channel plan** — focus on one platform (e.g., blog only, newsletter only).
- **Campaign sprint** — plan a concentrated burst of content around a launch or event.
- **Repurposing plan** — map how one core piece becomes multiple formats across channels.
- **Ongoing editorial calendar** — rolling plan that gets updated weekly or monthly.
