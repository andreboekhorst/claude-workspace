# Anticipate

Prepare thoroughly for questions by brainstorming what will be asked and drafting strong answers.

## Typical flow

1. **Identify** the topic, context, and setting — what are you presenting, and to whom?
2. **Brainstorm** likely questions — think from the audience's perspective, not your own
3. **Categorize** questions by theme — group related questions to spot patterns and gaps
4. **Draft** concise answers for each question — clear, honest, and complete
5. **Prepare** for follow-ups — identify where answers might invite deeper probing
6. **Flag** weak spots — mark questions where the answer is uncertain or the position is vulnerable
7. **Rehearse** the hardest questions — practice delivering answers for the toughest ones

## Inputs

- The topic or material being presented or defended
- The audience (who will be asking, what do they care about, what is their expertise?)
- The setting (presentation, interview, board meeting, panel, Q&A, defense)
- Optional: known concerns or controversial points
- Optional: areas where the user feels least prepared

## Outputs

- Q&A prep document organized by theme
- Each entry: likely question, drafted answer, and potential follow-ups
- Weak spots flagged with suggested strategies for handling them
- Optional: cheat sheet with key facts, figures, or talking points for quick reference

## Quality signals

- Questions reflect the audience's actual concerns, not softballs
- Answers are concise — they respond directly before adding context
- Follow-up chains go at least two levels deep on critical topics
- Weak spots are identified honestly, with a strategy beyond "hope it doesn't come up"
- The prep covers hostile, friendly, and unexpected question types

## Common variations

- **Media prep**: Short, quotable answers with bridge phrases and message discipline
- **Technical defense**: Deep-dive questions on methodology, data, and edge cases
- **Stakeholder Q&A**: Focus on impact, timeline, budget, and risk
- **Job interview**: Behavioral and situational questions mapped to experience
- **Panel discussion**: Broader questions with room for perspective and opinion
