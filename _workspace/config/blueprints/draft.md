# Draft

Turn a brief or rough idea into a complete, structured document ready for review.

## Typical flow

1. **Understand** the brief — clarify purpose, audience, format, and length expectations.
2. **Outline** the structure — propose sections, key points, and logical flow.
3. **Draft** the full document — write each section following the approved outline.
4. **Review** the draft — check coherence, tone, completeness, and alignment with the brief.
5. **Deliver** the finished document — present the clean version for the user's review.

## Inputs

- A brief, prompt, or rough idea describing what needs to be written.
- Target audience (who will read this).
- Desired format and approximate length.
- Tone or style preferences (if any).
- Reference material or examples (optional).

## Outputs

- A complete document ready for review.
- Optional: a short summary of choices made during drafting.

## Quality signals

- The document directly addresses every point in the brief.
- Structure is logical and easy to follow.
- Tone matches the stated audience and purpose.
- No filler — every paragraph earns its place.
- The user can move to review/edit without needing another rewrite.

## Common variations

- **Outline only** — stop after step 2 and deliver just the structure.
- **Section-by-section** — draft one section at a time, getting approval before continuing.
- **Multiple drafts** — produce two or three alternative approaches for the user to choose from.
- **Template-based** — start from an existing template or format the user provides.
