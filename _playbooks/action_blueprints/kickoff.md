# Kickoff

Guide the user through scoping a new project so that everyone involved starts with shared understanding of purpose, boundaries, and responsibilities.

## Typical flow

1. **Define** purpose — articulate why this project exists and what problem it solves.
2. **Set** scope and boundaries — clarify what is in scope, what is explicitly out.
3. **Identify** milestones — list the key deliverables and target dates.
4. **Assign** roles — determine who owns what (driver, contributors, reviewers).
5. **Flag** risks — surface assumptions, dependencies, and potential blockers.
6. **Compile** the brief — assemble everything into a kickoff document.

## Inputs

- Project idea or initiative description.
- Known stakeholders and their roles.
- Timeline expectations or hard deadlines.
- Any prior context: related projects, past attempts, existing assets.

## Outputs

- A kickoff document or project brief containing:
  - Purpose statement.
  - Scope (in/out).
  - Milestones with owners and dates.
  - Roles and responsibilities.
  - Risk register with mitigations.

## Quality signals

- Purpose is clear to someone outside the project.
- Scope boundaries prevent creep — "out of scope" is explicit.
- Every milestone has an owner.
- Risks include at least one mitigation each.
- The document is shareable and self-contained.

## Common variations

- **Solo project**: Skip roles, focus on scope and milestones.
- **Client project**: Add a section for client expectations and approval criteria.
- **Internal initiative**: Emphasize alignment with company goals and success metrics.
- **Lightweight**: One-pager format for small efforts that still need clarity.
