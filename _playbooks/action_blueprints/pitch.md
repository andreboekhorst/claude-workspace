# Pitch

Craft a persuasive, well-structured case that moves an audience toward a decision.

## Typical flow

1. **Identify** the audience and what they care about — their priorities, concerns, and decision criteria
2. **Define** the core proposition — one sentence that captures what you are asking for and why it matters
3. **Build** supporting arguments — evidence, examples, and reasoning that back the proposition
4. **Anticipate** objections — what will the audience push back on, and how will you address it?
5. **Craft** the narrative — sequence arguments into a compelling arc with a clear ask
6. **Sharpen** the opening and closing — the first 30 seconds and the final ask matter most
7. **Prepare** supporting materials — data, visuals, or demos that reinforce the case

## Inputs

- The idea, product, or change being pitched
- The target audience (who decides, who influences)
- The desired outcome (approval, funding, buy-in, next meeting, etc.)
- Optional: known objections or concerns to address
- Optional: format constraints (time limit, presentation vs. written, formal vs. informal)

## Outputs

- Pitch document or presentation outline with a clear narrative arc
- Core proposition statement (one sentence)
- Objection-response pairs for likely pushback
- Supporting evidence organized by argument
- Optional: slide outline, one-pager, or executive summary

## Quality signals

- The core proposition is clear within the first 30 seconds
- Every argument connects back to what the audience cares about, not just what the pitcher cares about
- Objections are addressed honestly, not dismissed or ignored
- The ask is specific and actionable — the audience knows exactly what to do next
- The pitch works without the pitcher in the room — it stands on its own

## Common variations

- **Elevator pitch**: 60 seconds, core proposition and one killer argument only
- **Investor pitch**: Problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask
- **Internal proposal**: Current state, proposed change, impact, resources needed, timeline
- **Sales pitch**: Pain point, solution, proof, pricing, next step
- **Change management**: Why now, what changes, who is affected, how we support the transition
